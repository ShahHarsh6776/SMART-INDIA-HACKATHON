Directive Principles of State Policy - Part IV of the Indian Constitution

Overview

The Directive Principles of State Policy, contained in Part IV of the Indian Constitution, are guidelines for the framing of laws by the government. These principles are not enforceable by any court, but they are considered fundamental in the governance of the country, aiming to create a social and economic democracy.

They act as a moral compass for the state, ensuring that policies are made to secure a just society where citizens have equal access to resources, and fundamental rights are upheld. Though not legally binding, the state is expected to keep these principles in mind while framing laws and policies.

List of Directive Principles

The Directive Principles aim to:

  • Secure adequate means of livelihood for all citizens.
  • Ensure the equitable distribution of material resources of the community for the common good.
  • Promote equal pay for equal work for men and women.
  • Protect the health and strength of workers and children.
  • Ensure that children receive free and compulsory education up to the age of 14 years.
  • Promote the participation of people in the administration of justice.
  • Promote cottage industries on an individual or cooperative basis in rural areas.
  • Secure a uniform civil code throughout the country.
  • Provide just and humane conditions for work and maternity relief.
  • Protect and improve the environment, forests, and wildlife of the country.
